Understanding the Blockchain

Cryptographic Linking

Each block contains the hash of the previous block, creating an immutable chain. Any attempt to modify past blocks would break the chain.

Immutability

Once a vote is recorded in a block, it cannot be altered or deleted without invalidating the entire chain, ensuring permanent record-keeping.

SHA-256 Hashing

Cryptographic hashing ensures that each block has a unique fingerprint. Even tiny changes would completely alter the hash.

Proof of Work

Each block requires computational work to be created, making it extremely difficult and expensive to forge or manipulate the chain.
